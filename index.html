Executive Summary

Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd (M&M) is a diversified automotive and farm equipment major with leadership positions across SUVs, tractors, and last-mile mobility, and a growing presence in electric vehicles. The company has delivered strong operating and financial performance, supported by premiumisation, market share gains in core segments, and a clear long-term electrification strategy.

The valuation in this report is carried out using a Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP) methodology to reflect the group’s diversified structure. The core automotive business is valued using a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model (mid-year convention), capturing long-term cash-flow generation driven by volume growth, margin expansion, and capital efficiency. Non-core investments and listed subsidiaries are valued separately based on their respective market capitalisations and M&M’s effective shareholding, with an appropriate holding company discount applied.

Company Overview

Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd. is the flagship company of the Mahindra Group, operating across Automotive, Farm Equipment, and Services. The company participates in sectors contributing nearly 70% of India’s GDP and remains deeply aligned with the country’s long-term growth story.

In FY25, M&M delivered record performance driven by strong demand in SUVs, tractors, and last-mile mobility, alongside rapid progress in its electric vehicle strategy under the “Born Electric” platform.

M&M has established a strategic target for electric vehicles (EVs) to constitute 25-30% of its total sales volume by 2027 to ensure alignment with CAFE 3 norms. Management indicates that a more conservative EV portfolio penetration of 15-25% would suffice for comfortable compliance, primarily due to the significant 'super credits' generated by zero-emission vehicles in the regulatory framework.

  • Standalone PAT (FY25): ₹11,855 crore (+11% YoY)

Industry Overview

The Indian automotive industry remains a primary engine of national economic growth, contributing over Rs. 20 lakh crore to the economy and accounting for approximately 15% of India's total GST collections. India is currently the third-largest passenger vehicle market globally and a global leader in the two-wheeler and three-wheeler segments.

The industry showed steady performance in FY 2024-25, with total production reaching 3.10 crore vehicles, up from 2.84 crore in the previous year.

Vehicles / SUVs:

• SUV Dominance: SUVs now account for 64-65% of overall PV volumes. ICRA expects this preference to persist, driving a projected 4-7% growth for the PV industry in FY 2026.

• Premiumization: There is a clear shift toward feature-rich, high-performance vehicles, with luxury electric vehicles seeing high demand.

Light Commercial Vehicles (LCV):

• The domestic LCV industry (cargo and passenger) recorded sales of 5.83 lakh units in FY 2024-25, representing a marginal de-growth of 2.0% compared to 5.95 lakh units in FY 2023-24.

Tractors & Farm Equipment:

• The Indian tractor industry achieved domestic sales of 9,39,713 units in FY 2024-25, registering a solid growth of 7.3%.

• Experts project that to achieve national foodgrain self-sufficiency, India’s tractor population must grow from the current 8.4 million to 17 million units by 2030.

Key Assumptions

Revenue Growth

Revenue growth assumptions are driven by volume expansion across SUVs, tractors, and electric vehicles, supported by stable market share, favourable product mix, and gradual improvement in realisations.

Margin Expansion

EBITDA margin expansion is assumed on account of operating leverage, premiumisation in the SUV portfolio, cost optimisation initiatives, and improving scale benefits in electric vehicle operations.

Capital Expenditure

Capital expenditure assumptions reflect continued investments in new product development, EV platforms, and capacity expansion, while maintaining capital discipline in mature segments.

Working Capital & Cash Flows

Working capital intensity is assumed to remain broadly stable, with improvements driven by inventory optimisation and disciplined receivables management, supporting healthy free cash flow generation.

Valuation Methodology

The company is valued using a Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP) approach. The core automotive business is valued using a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) methodology, while subsidiaries are valued based on market capitalisation adjusted for effective ownership and holding company discount.
